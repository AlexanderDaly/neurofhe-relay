## Strategic Readout

The FTO extraction indicates that a broad concept framed as "blockchain plus encrypted neural data plus smart-contract access control" is crowded and likely difficult to patent defensibly. The strongest ENER drafting posture is therefore to avoid treating blockchain storage, smart-contract permissions, decentralized retrieval, or encrypted records management as the inventive center.

ENER should instead emphasize privacy-preserving latent neural inference:

- Local latent compression before encryption.
- Encrypted embeddings rather than encrypted raw EEG or raw neural records.
- Homomorphic or privacy-preserving inference on compressed neural embeddings.
- Split-device inference architectures.
- Reconstruction-resistant cognitive embeddings.
- Adaptive neurocompression based on encryption cost, bandwidth, entropy, signal quality, and task type.

## Saturated Claim Territory to Avoid

The following concepts appear heavily covered and should not be the center of the ENER filing:

- Encrypted neural data on blockchain.
- Smart-contract access to neural data.
- Blockchain medical records.
- Encrypted EEG storage.
- Encrypted sensitive data with decentralized access permissions.
- Blockchain-coordinated decryption authorization.
- Off-chain encrypted storage address retrieval.
- Identity-gated encrypted record access.

## Stronger Claim Territory

Potentially stronger ENER claim territory includes:

- Adaptive latent compression optimized for FHE inference.
- Neural embedding relay protocols.
- Encrypted latent-space cognitive classification.
- Edge-device embedding generation before any storage, relay, or external interaction.
- Privacy-preserving neural foundation-model interfaces.
- Semantic-preserving encrypted embedding transport.
- Dynamic embedding sparsification for encrypted inference.
- Homomorphic operations on compressed neural manifolds.
- Federated encrypted neural embedding workflows.
- Identity-separated cognitive-state inference.

## Drafting Guidance for ENER Package

- Treat blockchain, smart contracts, and decentralized storage as optional infrastructure only if needed, not as the inventive concept.
- Keep the specification centered on local neural signal processing, latent embedding generation, compression policy, encryption of the embedding, and protected inference.
- Prefer "inference-before-storage" language where storage questions arise.
- If a later embodiment mentions distributed infrastructure, distinguish distributed encrypted compute from blockchain storage and access-control systems.
- In prior-art comparison, record whether a reference performs remote inference on encrypted latent embeddings or merely stores and retrieves encrypted sensitive data.
- In claim seeds, preserve the core sequence: acquire neural telemetry, locally generate a compressed latent embedding, encrypt the embedding, perform encrypted inference, output a result without exposing raw neural telemetry.
