# ENER Publication-Ready Formatting Guide

## Editorial Position

Use ENER as the acronym after first reference. The preferred full title is:

Encrypted Neural Embedding Relay (ENER): A Privacy-Preserving Architecture for Secure Neuroinference

The preferred category label is "privacy-preserving neural computation infrastructure."

Avoid public-facing phrases that imply generalized cognition decoding, surveillance capability, or medical efficacy. Use concrete systems language: neural telemetry, latent embedding, edge encoder, encrypted inference, local trust boundary, reconstruction-risk testing, metadata leakage, and cryptographic inventory.

## Tone

The style should resemble an institutional R&D briefing. Use measured claims, plain definitions, and bounded examples. Avoid hype language, crisis framing, and dramatic metaphors. Every section should distinguish what the architecture can do today from what would require validation.

## Layout Recommendations

- Cover page: title, subtitle, author line, date, document class, and short discussion-draft disclaimer.
- Executive summary: two pages maximum in formatted PDF.
- Body sections: numbered headings, 1-3 short paragraphs per subsection, selective tables.
- Diagrams: use black-and-white line diagrams suitable for policy printing.
- Tables: use sparingly for comparison, roadmap, and risk mapping.
- Appendix: glossary, acronyms, diagrams, claim seeds, references, and standards work.

## Citation Style

Use IEEE-style bracket citations in the body. The current package uses source-category references as placeholders for later publication review. Before public release, replace categories with full bibliographic entries and URLs.

## Language Replacements

| Avoided Framing Category | Prefer |
|---|---|
| sensational cognition language | privacy-preserving neural computation |
| surveillance-like cognition framing | bounded neuroinference |
| sensational storage metaphors | local neural privacy boundary |
| decentralized neural data marketplace | governed research collaboration infrastructure |
| strategic superiority framing | secure high-consequence human-computer interaction |
| medical diagnosis | clinical or neurodiagnostic use subject to validation |
| absolute reconstruction guarantees | designed and tested to reduce reconstruction risk |
| absolute privacy guarantees | privacy-preserving under defined assumptions |

## Publication Checks

Before release, verify citations, replace bracketed author fields, remove filing-only content, confirm no confidential patent strategy is disclosed, and have regulatory, privacy, security, and IP reviewers inspect the final draft.
