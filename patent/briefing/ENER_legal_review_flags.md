# ENER Statements for Legal, Regulatory, and Publication Review

The following statement categories should be reviewed before public release.

## Patent and IP Review

Statements that describe ENER as foundational infrastructure, patentable territory, standards-related positioning, or licensing strategy should be reviewed for privilege, public-disclosure timing, and claim-scope implications. Public language should not concede obviousness, overstate novelty, or disclose implementation details that should remain reserved for patent filings.

## Regulatory Review

Any statement suggesting medical, neurodiagnostic, assistive-control, rehabilitation, or clinical use should be reviewed for intended-use implications. The briefing should maintain that clinical applications require validation and regulatory assessment. Avoid language that implies FDA clearance, diagnostic accuracy, treatment effect, or safety.

## Privacy and Civil-Liberties Review

Statements about cognitive privacy, workplace use, education, national security, and high-consequence human-computer interaction should be reviewed for policy sensitivity. The draft should emphasize safeguards, voluntary use, use limitation, transparency, and governance.

## Security Review

Statements about encryption, reconstruction resistance, secure inference, and protected computation should be checked against actual implementation status. Avoid absolute privacy, security, or reconstruction-resistance guarantees. Use "under defined assumptions" and disclose limitations.

## FTO and Competitive Review

References to blockchain, smart contracts, decentralized access management, encrypted medical records, or encrypted data storage should be minimized or framed as outside the core ENER invention. The FTO extraction indicates that this area is crowded.

## Publication Review

Before release, replace generic citation categories with full references, confirm all dates and statutory descriptions, remove internal filing notes, and confirm whether the provisional application has been filed if publication timing matters for foreign rights.
